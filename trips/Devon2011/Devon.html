Devon 2011

 

At the end of August we travelled to Devon for a short break. We didn’t know Devon, having previously either stopped in Somerset or continued through to Cornwall.

We decided to split our time in Devon between the south coast and the north coast, so we spent four nights in Dartmouth and three nights just outside Barnstaple. We explored a little of the area around each location and travelled across part of Dartmoor to get from one to the other. However, this means that we only saw a very little of Devon.

Townstall Farmhouse Hotel in Dartmouth where we stayed. We left the car in the car park and walked, used ferries and busses.

Browns Hill, part of the ‘gentle’ route down to and up from the town!

After Arlington Court we went to the coast and walked the mile to Baggy Point on the tip of Croyde Bay. Then returned on the top path.

Dartmouth Quay

The ferry from Dartmouth to Kingswear, the other side of the river.

The lower car ferry. There were two; each held eight cars and were moved by a tug.

We took a ferry up river to Greenway, the former holiday home of Agatha Christie and now owned by the National Trust. It was about a half hour journey and then a steep climb up to the house.

The Britannia Naval College, as seen from the ferry.

Greenway

The boathouse, firstly the view from it and then a view of it from the ferry.

That morning, the Ocean Princess cruise ship was moored in the middle of the river. It was just casting off when we arrived back. The whole of Dartmouth seemed to be on the quay to see it leave.

We took the ferry across to Kingswear and followed a five-mile figure of eight walk through the woods along the river. These photos show Dartmouth from Kingswear, part of the path and the Naval College seen from across the river.

Part of the walk was alongside the Paignton-Dartmouth Steam Railway. Brunel built the line and wanted to bridge the river to take the railway into Dartmouth. He even built a station on the quay. However, he could not get permission to bridge the river and so the station is now a cafe!

One day we took the bus from Dartmouth to Kingsbridge, a very pleasant ride along the coast. We had planned to then take a ferry down to Salcombe, but the tide was out and so the creek at Kingsbridge had no water!

The old town hall and the Shambles (shops under a canopy) at Kingsbridge. The Shambles were once in the middle of the road, but were moved so as to let traffic pass.

On our way from Dartmouth to Barnstaple we stopped at Castle Drogo on Dartmoor. It is a Victorian castle, designed by Lutchens for a wealthy industrialist and now owned by the National Trust.

Although only on the edge of Dartmoor, Castle Drogo has some spectacular views.

We stayed for three nights at Lower Yelland Farm, a farmhouse built around a quadrangle that now included holiday cottages and a B&B. It was still a working farm, rearing beef cattle.

We visited Rosemore, the RHS gardens to the north of Barnstaple.

And also Arlington Court, now owned by the National Trust, but the former home of the Chichester family.

Arlington Court also houses the National Trust’s carriage museum. They keep four horses that they use to give harnessing demonstrations and carriage rides. This is Erik.
