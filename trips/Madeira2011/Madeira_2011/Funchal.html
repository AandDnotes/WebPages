Funchal

 

Funchal is the capital of Madeira and cascades down the hillside to the sea. It has a port, a marina and a collection of narrow streets in various parts of the old town.

The worker’s market is very active. Madeira grows most of its own fruit and vegetables and catches most of its fish. It is here where the produce is brought for sale.


Since the climate is very moderate, you find cabbages growing under banana palms and potatoes growing next to oranges and pineapples.

Either side of Funchal the coast becomes more rugged, although street cafes for lunch can still be found. There is a good promenade path from the Lido area (where we were staying) westward.
