A View of Eastern Madeira

 

The eastern side of Madeira is said to be the ‘pretty’ side, with forested hills and ravines running down to the sea. We took a coach tour, starting from Funchal and working our way to the north coast.

Our first stop was at Ribeiro Frio (Cold River). It was chilly, but there were spectacular views. We took a short walk beside a very narrow irrigation channel known as a levada (more about these in Lavada Walking).

After Ribeiro Frio we made a short stop above Faial before moving on to ....

... view some Palheiro houses at Santana on the north coast. These are traditional Madeiran houses, but only a few are left. There were two that have been restored by the tourist board and one that was lived in (although most of it was a modern extension).

We stopped for lunch at a modern restaurant (with a swimming pool) that was run by the Madeira government at Sao Jorge, in a narrow ravine on the rocky coast.

We then returned to Funchal via Porto da Cruz and Machico.
