Monte

 

As the crow flies, Monte (with a silent e) is about three and a half kilometres north of Funchal, but 600 metres higher. Until the 1940’s, Monte was connected to Funchal by a cog railway. However, a locomotive exploded, killing some people, and now, apart from the road, there is a cable car.

A 15 minute ride takes you from near the promenade in Funchal up to Monte, with expanding views of Funchal all the way. However, the view from Monte itself is the best.

Monte is probably most famous for its ‘toboggan run’! At one time produce from Monte was taken down to market in Funchal in wicker baskets on wooden runners. At the time the roads were cobbled and so the two ‘drivers’ could get the basket going at quite a speed.

Today the streets are paved with tarmac, the contents of the baskets are tourists and the ride only goes half way to Funchal.

Otherwise, there is a church (Our Lady of Monte), some public gardens and terraces of houses.
