At the beginning of September we spent a week in South Wales. We made multiple stops as we worked our way across to Pembroke and back again. After a short pause in Bracknell we spent our first night just outside Chepstow before heading to the north of the Brecon Beacons (through Brecon) to a small hotel at Cothi Bridge. We stopped at Dinefwr park on the way but really stayed in this are so as to visit the National Botanic Garden of Wales.

From there we went west, via Carmarthen and Haverfordwest, to St Davids and to Solva before staying in a farmhouse at Johnston. We had an excellent walk around part of the Pembokeshire coast at Stackpole and visited Tenby.

Our return journey took us firstly to Aberdulais Falls, just outside Neath before taking the ‘head  of the valleys’ road, coming down Ebbw Vale to Newport, where we spent out last night in Wales.

We broke our journey home from Newport by stopping at Mottisfont Abbey in Hampshire.
