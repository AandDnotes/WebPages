Another year when we limited our visits to places to whatever could be achieved in a day. On the one hand Angela’s mother has become less able to fend for herself, often deciding to stay in her dressing gown, and on the other, Pip has become more neurotic and less able to be left for any length of time.

So we only travelled relatively locally and, by the end of the year, for no more than half a day.

Leonardslee gardens closed to the public this year, so we made a final short visit. Since Angela’s mother and aunt were with us we were not able to explore the valleys and lakes.

Grays Court, just west of Henley-on-Thames, is a National Trust property that we had not visited before. So we met friends there for our annual picnic. We only needed to put the umbrellas up once while eating our lunch!

Chartwell in Kent was a good day out. The interior of the house, particularly Winston Churchill’s study, was very interesting. There were many visitors and Churchill’s studio was very popular.

High Beeches near Handcross in West Sussex is a local garden that we had often talked of visiting. It is relatively small, being formed around a small valley. It had a good display of gentians and a good tea shop.

At the beginning of November, in fact the day before the London to Brighton vintage car rally, I took an early morning bus ride to Brighton. The event was the inaugural Brighton to London ‘Car of the Future Challenge’.

A variety of production and prototype electric, fuel cell, hybrid and fuel efficient vehicles set off from Madeira Drive, to see how little energy they needed to get to London.

I was particularly interested in the conversion of this blue MGF to electric power, since I drive an MGF. The yellow Lotus Elise was also a private conversion to electric power.

This is one of three Tesla electric sports cars that took part. They are production cars, based on another Lotus model, and very pricey.
