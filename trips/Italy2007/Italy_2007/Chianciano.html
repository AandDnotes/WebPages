Chianciano is a spa town where you can take the water to help your liver! The old town is a typical Tuscan hill-top town but the lower slopes are filled with hotels and, of course, the spas. We used Chianciano as a base from which to travel to Sienna and Florence and then to Assisi on our way to Rome.

Our flight from Gatwick was delayed by about 45 minutes and so it arrived later in Rome than had been expected. Rather than wait for our evening meal until we reached Chianciano we decided to stop at a motorway service station for something to eat. It was quite a different experience (better) than visiting a UK service station. On Wednesday evening most of the party took the coach just down the road (in the middle of nowhere) to a restaurant for a 'typical' Tuscan meal. Anti-pasti, then a pasta course followed by a main course of wild boar sausages and beef casserole. All with plenty of red wine from unlabelled bottles. The mousse-like dessert was followed by a variety of liqueurs, including grappa. The following evening, Thursday, we eat in the hotel. The same format but less in the way of atmosphere (and wine/liqueurs).
