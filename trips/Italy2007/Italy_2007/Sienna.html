On Wednesday morning we set off fairly early for a one and a half hour coach journey to Sienna. We left the coach below what had been a fort and walked to the Basilica of San Domenico for a comfort break and to meet our guide, Nicolette. It seems that only officially licenced guides can give tours of Italian cities. Within the Basilica there is the Capella di Santa Caterina (Chapel of St. Catherine), complete with the mummified head and finger of St. Catherine! (Her foot is in Avignon and her body in Rome.) We did not linger.

We continued our tour, firstly on to the main square (which isn't) and then on to the Duomo (the Cathedral). It seems that Sienna is made up of a number of 17 Contrada, or city wards, each with the name of an animal such as Elephant, Tortoise, Caterpillar and Unicorn. The inhabitants of Sienna 'belong' to the area in which they were born (or where there parents lived when they were born) and retain an allegiance to this area even if they move or marry. Our guide, Nicolette, was a Caterpillar, whereas her husband was an Elephant.

The colours of Sienna are black and white. Even though the dark marble on the outside of the Duomo and its tower is dark green, the Siennese say it is a 'black green'. The outside is impressive but the queue to get inside was too long for the time we had available.

On our way back to Chianciano we stopped at a small hill town called Pienza. The streets were picturesque and there were panoramic views over the valley below. Pienza was used as the location for some of the scenes in the film The English Patient. They also had a lot of small shops selling Tuscan produce such as olive oil.

Twice a year there is a horse race called Il Palio around Il Campo, with each area entering a draw for the chance to enter (there are more areas than the maximum number of horses that can race). The ground is covered with a thick layer of sand and the centre of Il Campo is filled with spectators. The race, three laps of Il Campo, takes only a few minutes, but then the winning area celebrates for four days! La Torre del Mangia (the tower) and the Palazzo Pubblico (Town Hall) are at the foot of Il Campo. We queued for 15 minutes to go up the the tower but in that time the front of the queue did not move, so we gave up and went for an ice-cream.

It seems that the Siennese have a particular dislike of the Florentines. The city state of Florence once invaded Sienna and now the Siennese prefer not to even say the name of Fierenze (Florence) only being prepared to take their money!
