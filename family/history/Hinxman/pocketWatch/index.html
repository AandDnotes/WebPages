Pocket Watch

In June 1951, Percy William Hinxman, Angela’s grandfather, bought a pocket watch from Bravingtons in Pentonville Road, Kings Cross, London. It cost him £25, which, according to the Bank of England, was the equivalent of £667 in November 2024. At the time, Percy and his wife Rose were living at 2 Longfield Avenue, Hackbridge, Walington in Surrey. This is just west of Croydon.

In 2024 we asked Wheelers in Worthing if they were interested in buying it and also how much it would cost to service it. They said that they would offer us £385 for it in its non-working state and that a service (including changing the front crystal) would cost £395. Since we have all the provenance to go with the watch we decided to have it serviced and keep it in the family.

The hand-written text on the receipt seems to say ‘Gents solid gold 17 jewel pocket watch’.
